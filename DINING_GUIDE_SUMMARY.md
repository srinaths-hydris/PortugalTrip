## 🌟 Must-Visit Restaurants (Already Specified)

### Porto
- **Pastéis de nata**: Lello Bookshop Café (after Harry Potter bookshop)
- **Francesinha**: Café Santiago, Rua de Passos Manuel 226
- **Dinner**: Brasão Aliados, Rua de Sá da Bandeira 16

### Lisbon
- **Pastéis de Belém**: Rua de Belém 84-92 — THE original since 1837! 🥐
  - **NON-NEGOTIABLE!** Go early (by 9:30 AM) to avoid lines
  - Kids will go CRAZY for warm custard tarts
- **Time Out Market**: Av. 24 de Julho 49
  - Visiting TWICE (Days 8 & 9) — everyone picks their own vendor!
  - Perfect solution for picky eaters

### Sintra
- **Piriquita**: Rua das Padarias 1
  - Travesseiros (pillow pastries) and queijadas (cheese tarts)
  - Sintra specialties - grab before heading to palaces

### Algarve
- **Day 11 Welcome Dinner**: PLACEHOLDER - seafood restaurant in Armação de Pêra
  - Celebration dinner with D&R (6 people!)
  - Book ahead

## 👶 Kid-Friendly Tips from Guide

1. **Meia dose** (half portions) are GENEROUS — share!
2. Plain pasta with olive oil = available everywhere
3. Fresh bread always on table (free)
4. Ice cream shops = strategic bribes
5. Portuguese love kids — high chairs widely available
6. Soup (sopa) = usually kid-approved
7. Pastéis de nata = instant kid happiness

## 📱 How to Scout on the Go

1. Open Google Maps
2. Search "restaurants near me"
3. Filter by:
   - Rating: 4.0+
   - Recent reviews (past 3 months)
   - "Kid-friendly" tag
4. Look at photos of food (not just decor)
5. Check if they have outdoor seating (kids can move around)
6. Verify hours (some close 3-5 PM between lunch/dinner)

## 🚨 Red Flags to Avoid

- ❌ Tourist menus in 5 languages with photos
- ❌ Aggressive touts outside trying to pull you in
- ❌ Empty restaurants at peak hours (locals know!)
- ❌ Located RIGHT on main tourist square (walk 2 blocks away)
- ✅ Locals eating there = GOOD SIGN
- ✅ Menu in Portuguese only = Often the best
- ✅ Simple decor, focus on food = Authentic
