# Aly Smalls' Portugal Travel Blog: 12 Day Itinerary & Highlights

**Source:** Travel blog reference for Portugal trip planning

---

## Overview

12 days exploring Portugal from sun-kissed beaches in the Algarve to historic cities like Lisbon and Porto, as well as enchanting regions like the Douro Valley and Sintra.

Perfect for first-time visitors to catch all of the highlights with a few hidden gems sprinkled in.

**Trip Style:** Not sponsored – full price paid for everything (hotels, tours, meals, etc). Special trip with mother and sister.

**Route:** Flew into Porto, out of Faro (no backtracking!)

---

## The Places Visited

### Porto
Ultra charming with riverside views, iconic bridges, and port wine cellars.
- Wander through medieval-esque Ribeira district
- Cool viewpoints overlooking terra-cotta rooftops
- Try port from where it was invented
- Sunset views from Jardim do Morro

### Douro Valley
Famed wine region not far from Porto, with historic wine estates among breathtaking views of rolling hills that hug the Douro River.

### Lisbon
Vibrant capital blending history and modern culture:
- Alfama's winding alleys
- Castle views
- Belém's iconic monastery and tower
- Pastéis de nata
- Scenic miradouros across seven hills
- Traditional Fado music

### Sintra
Fairytale-like town surrounded by lush forests and extravagant palaces.

### Algarve Coast
Southern Portugal dazzles with golden beaches, dramatic cliffs, and charming fishing villages:
- Praia da Marinha
- Ponta da Piedade's sea caves and arches
- Iconic Benagil cave

## Days 1-4: Porto and Douro Valley

**2.5 days in Porto + 1 day trip to Douro Valley**

### Porto Highlights

#### Walking Food Tour
Started first full day with food tour – AWESOME! Helped get acquainted with food and cultural intricacies.

#### Mercado do Bolhão
Original open air market with all kinds of vendors. Get wine while you shop – fun & lively atmosphere.

#### Wandering Ribeira District
- **Don't miss:** Largo da Pena Ventosa (cutest corner in old town!)
- Flores street – live music and lots of patios

#### Wine Caves in Vila Nova de Gaia
Walk across river via Luís I Bridge and do port wine tastings.
- **Recommended:** Caves Calem and Cockburn's Porto Lodge (2 very different experiences)

#### Fado Show
Taberna Real do Fado – unforgettable experience.
- Set menu, doors open 8pm
- Meal service pauses for show
- 2 minute walk from hotel
- Highly recommend!

#### VW Bus City Tour
Open-air VW bus tour – really fun!
- Guide took to beach, hidden gem cathedrals, viewpoints never would have found

### Douro Valley Day Trip
**One of favorite days of whole trip!**

Small group tour led by sommelier:
- Visited 2 wineries (each very different)
- Stopped in adorable town of Pinhão
- Boat ride down Douro River
- Tasty lunch with plenty of wine
- Group was only 8 people – personalized and fun

**Booked on:** Get Your Guide

### Must Try Foods & Drinks in Porto
- **Vinho Verde wine** (green/young wine)
- **Porto-tonic** (local take on classic cocktail)
- **Francesinha** (bread, meat, cheese, egg, tomato-beer sauce)
- **Pastel de nata** – loved Castro the most
- **Octopus and cod**

### Highlight Restaurants
- **Rooftop Flores** – amazing hidden gem garden terrace, great for sunset drinks
- **Brasão Aliados** – where they tried Francesinha
- **Cantina 32** – close to hotel, fun vibe, quality Portuguese dishes, great for sharing

### Where We Stayed: Vitoria's Terrace Apartments
**Rating:** 11/10 – phenomenal spot!

**Why it was great:**
- Perfect location – walkable to everywhere in old town, yet quiet at night
- Amazing view from balcony
- Next door to hidden gem viewpoint (had this view from private balcony!)
- Fully equipped room with cooking provisions
- Lovely couple runs the apart-hotel – incredibly kind and helpful with local recommendations

## Day 5: Historic Villages (Porto to Lisbon)

**Travel day made into sightseeing day**

Hired driver through Viator – only a few hundred dollars more than train, turned travel day into entire day of sightseeing.

Picked up 9am, arrived Lisbon 6pm.

### Aveiro
Nicknamed "Venice of Portugal" – canals and brightly colored moliceiros (traditional boats). Beautiful buildings in Art Nouveau style.

### Nazaré
Traditional fishing village with highest waves on earth (can reach 30 meters, usually December-March). Picturesque village with pink marble cathedral as highlight.

### Óbidos
**Really loved this one – would have spent half a day here!**

One of Portugal's most well-preserved medieval villages:
- Narrow streets
- Walk on top of old castle walls
- Capital of Ginja (cherry liquor served in chocolate cup for ~1 euro)
- Great souvenir shopping
- Lovely sites and restaurants
- Old castle is now a 4-star hotel

**Day 5 thought:** Would have skipped Aveiro to spend more time in Óbidos.

## Days 6 & 7: Lisbon

Arrived and checked into hotel.

### First Day
Started with guided tuk tuk tour – perfect way to get acquainted with main areas.
- 4 hours with guide
- Amazing viewpoints, cathedrals, Belém
- Interesting history about city and Portugal
- Learned and saw SO much

**Booked on:** Get Your Guide

### Lisbon Highlights
- **Alfama neighborhood** – Lisbon's oldest (dating to 1150), maze of narrow medieval streets, small squares, breathtaking viewpoints
- **Chiado and Bairro Alto neighborhoods**
- **Viewpoints (Miradouros):** Graça, Portas do Sol, Santa Luzia, Senhora do Monte
- **Belém** – land of pastries and monuments (truly the best pastel de nata!)
- **Carmo rooftop bar** – amazing view (wine, charcuterie, pizza)
- **Green street** (at hotel) – tons of cute wine bars and restaurants
- **Casa do Pastel de Nata Licor** – espresso martinis Lisbon-style
- **Fado Show at Bohemia LX** – another incredible evening!

### Overrated in Lisbon
- **Time Out Market** – just a food court
- **Pink Street** – loud, busy, full of influencers
- **Waiting in line for Santa Justa lift** – cool to look at, great view from Carmo rooftop

### Where We Stayed: Ando Living – Abrantes 10 House
**4 nights**

**Why it was great:**
- Perfect location
- Around corner from "green street" (3 blocks of authentic bars/restaurants)
- Similar to Pink Street but better, more authentic, hidden gem among locals
- Apart-hotel with fully equipped kitchen, washing machine, 2 balconies
- Clean, reasonably priced, easy to walk everywhere
- Welcomed with wine and goodies

## Day 8: Sintra

Full-day tour – one of most popular day trips, shouldn't be missed.

Charming, fairy-tale town nestled in hills just outside Lisbon. Romantic architecture, lush landscapes, mystical atmosphere.

### What We Visited
**Pena Palace** – whimsically romantic, gift from King of Austria who married Queen of Portugal

**Quinta da Regaleira** – intricate palace with enchanting gardens and hidden wells

**Azenhas do Mar** – "Portugal's Santorini"

### Tour Notes
**Booked private tour – absolutely worth every penny.**

Guide:
- Brought everywhere and explained history/stories
- Helped navigate crowds
- Showed best places efficiently
- Many people without guides tried to listen to our guide!

**Wish:** Had time for Moorish Castle

**Booked through:** Daniel Ponce on his website (tell him Aly Smalls sent you!)

## Days 9-12: The Algarve (Southern Portugal)

Flew Lisbon to Faro. Rented car at airport, drove to home-base in Carvoeiro (4 nights).

**Location notes:**
- Faro to Lagos = ~2 hour drive
- Carvoeiro perfectly in middle
- Charming village with dramatic cliffs, peaceful beaches, relaxed atmosphere
- Lively restaurants and bars in town

### Where We Stayed: Quinta do Ourives
**6-suite guesthouse bed & breakfast**

**Rating:** 100% will book again!

**Why it was great:**
- Run by sweetest family, kind and full of recommendations
- Massive breakfast spread every morning, any coffee you want
- Large heated pool with sunbathing room
- Common areas to relax
- All rooms have private patios with kitchens
- Optimal location: 10 min to restaurants, 5 min to beach/hiking, 45 min to Faro airport or Lagos

**Note:** Only had 4 nights, wished for 10 more!

### Algarve Highlights

#### Algar Seco & Carvoeiro Boardwalk
Fantastic hidden gem, 5 minutes from hotel. Incredible views, cliffs, sea caves you can climb around.
- **Tip:** Beneco Bar – cool spot for drink between rocks

#### Seven Hanging Valleys Trail
**One of highlights of trip!**

8km hiking trail starting near Carvoeiro, most scenic in Algarve. Runs along cliffs with stunning coastal views and hidden beaches below.

**Tips:**
- Start of trail only 6 min from hotel
- Park at Praia do Vale de Centeanes, Uber to start at Praia da Marinha
- Public restrooms at first beach (~3km mark) and beach restaurants for break
- More restrooms at end – celebration beer at O Stop patio

#### Kayaking Benagil Caves
Really cool experience. Kayaked into several caves, got much closer than boat tours.
- **Note:** Can only be booked online
- **Booked through:** Get Your Guide

#### Ponta da Piedade
Coastal rock formations, sea caves, grottos. One of most famous photo spots of Algarve.

#### Pool Time
Couldn't resist relaxing day poolside at guesthouse!

#### Beach Afternoon
Algarve beaches like nowhere else!
- **Loved:** Praia dos Estudantes and Praia da Dona Ana (most famous for a reason)

#### Strolling Through Ferragudo
Adorably picturesque town with white-washed houses built into hillside, full of bougainvillea.
- Fun little place to shop

## Key Takeaways & Tips

### What Worked Well
- Flying into Porto, out of Faro (no backtracking)
- Food tour on first day in Porto
- Hiring driver for Porto-Lisbon journey
- Private tour in Sintra
- Small group tours (8 people)
- Staying in apart-hotels with kitchens

### What Would Change
- Skip Aveiro, spend more time in Óbidos
- More time at Quinta do Ourives in Algarve

### Worth the Money
- Private tours (Sintra)
- Small group experiences (Douro Valley)
- Hiring driver for scenic route

### Overrated
- Time Out Market Lisbon
- Pink Street Lisbon
- Santa Justa lift line
